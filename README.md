# FUTURE_DS_03
 📊 Title
*Marketing Funnel & Conversion Performance Analysis 
## 📝 Description
This dashboard provides a comprehensive view of marketing performance metrics between *January 1, 2019 and December 3, 2019*. It consolidates data on spending, discounts, coupon usage, tenure months, and product category pricing. The visualizations highlight geographic spending distribution, product category contributions, and coupon effectiveness, offering insights into both online and offline marketing channels.
## 🔑 Key Insights
- *Spending Overview*
  - *Total Spending:* $1.55M  
  - *Offline Spend:* $925,400 (dominant channel)  
  - *Online Spend:* $625,274.58  

- *Discounts & Coupons*
  - *Total Discounts:* 4,080 units applied  
  - Coupon usage shows varying effectiveness: some clicked but not used, others fully redeemed.  
  - Higher discount percentages (20–30%) correlate with increased coupon engagement.

- *Customer Tenure*
  - *Total Tenure Months:* 9,024  
  - Spending is distributed across tenure ranges, with notable peaks in mid-tenure customers (20–40 months).

- *Geographic Spending*
  - Strong spending in *Chicago, California, and New York*, with green bars indicating growth compared to other regions.  
  - Washington DC and New Jersey show relatively lower contributions.

- *Product Categories*
  - *Top Categories by Avg Price Contribution:* Nest-USA, Apparel, Bags, and Gift Cards.  
  - Lifestyle and Drinkware contribute less to overall average pricing.
## 📌 Conclusion
The dashboard reveals that *offline channels drive the majority of spending, while **online channels still represent a significant share*. Coupon campaigns are moderately effective, with higher discounts encouraging engagement but not guaranteeing usage. Mid-tenure customers appear to be the most valuable segment, suggesting loyalty programs could further enhance retention. Geographically, Chicago, California, and New York are strong markets, while Washington DC and New Jersey may need targeted strategies. Product category analysis highlights Nest-USA and Apparel as premium contributors, indicating opportunities to expand offerings in these segment.
